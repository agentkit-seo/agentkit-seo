# LinkedIn experience and skills

> This file outlines how to structure your professional history and skills on LinkedIn to maintain semantic consistency with your CV and maximize algorithmic ranking.

---

## 1. Overview

The Experience and Skills sections serve as the evidentiary foundation of your profile. They validate the claims made in your Headline and About sections. Inconsistencies here—especially when compared to a submitted CV—can trigger automated rejections or human distrust.

## 2. Experience constraints

**Rule:** Avoid the "Trust Gap" by keeping the CV and LinkedIn in sync.
Ensure the dates, job titles, and core impacts listed on your LinkedIn Experience match the facts on your ATS-optimized CV. Automated background check tools and recruiters flag significant semantic discrepancies as a "Trust Gap," which can lead to immediate rejection.

**Rule:** Use the Action + Metric format for bullet points.
Just like your CV, use formulas like STAR or XYZ (e.g., "Optimized SQL queries, reducing server costs by 20%"). Do not write generic paragraphs outlining your basic duties.

**Rule:** Map direct URLs into Experience entries.
For technical roles, do not just list the projects you worked on. Add direct links to the relevant GitHub repositories or live product demos within the specific Experience entry. AI scrapers use these explicit links as hard proof of work.

## 3. Skills constraints

**Rule:** Prioritize Verified Skill Assessments over peer endorsements.
Historically, peer endorsements drove search ranking. Today, LinkedIn's algorithms (and external recruiters) heavily weight **Verified Skill Assessments** (the badges you earn by taking LinkedIn's internal quizzes). You must pass the assessments for your top 3-5 core programming languages or tools.

**Rule:** Pin your top 3 skills to match your headline.
You can pin your top 3 skills to the top of the Skills section. These must perfectly align with the keywords in your Headline and About section to reinforce the profile's overall semantic coherence for the 360brew algorithm.

**Rule:** Maximize the 100-skill limit.
LinkedIn expanded the profile skills limit from 50 to 100. Do not leave this half empty. Fill it with a broad range of technical keywords, soft skills, and industry terminology to guarantee you cluster correctly in AI agent vector searches.

## 4. Anti-Patterns

### The orphan skill

**What it looks like:** Listing 100 skills in the Skills section, but failing to mention the top 5 anywhere in the Experience or About sections.
**Why it fails:** The 360brew algorithm looks for "Semantic Coherence." If a skill is listed in isolation without context, it carries significantly less weight in search rankings.
**What to do instead:** Ensure your pinned top skills are woven naturally into your Experience bullet points.
